Sources & assumptions: Engagement: Gallup State of the Global Workplace 2025 — only 21% of employees globally are engaged; disengagement costs ~18% of salary in lost productivity; 70% of team engagement variance is attributable to the manager. This model assumes only 7% of disengaged employees improve — below the Gallup benchmark. Retention: Gallup 2025 found 42% of exits are preventable; we use a conservative 10% reduction. Replacement cost: SHRM 2025 — 50–200% of annual salary (model defaults to 50%). Manager effectiveness: Gallup 2025 links manager engagement directly to $438B in lost global productivity; model uses only 1% team output lift across avg team of 7. Operational drivers (engagement + manager effectiveness) are adjusted by 0.7× to reduce potential overlap in affected employee populations. Retention savings are kept separate and unadjusted as direct cost avoidance.  Even partial adoption or uneven execution can create measurable value; this model does not assume perfect rollout.  Hard savings such as retention typically appear earlier, while productivity and manager-effectiveness gains build over time.  Delaying action preserves current cost exposure without reducing underlying risk.
